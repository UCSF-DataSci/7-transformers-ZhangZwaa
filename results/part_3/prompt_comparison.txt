## Question: What foods should be avoided by patients with gout?

### Zero-shot response:
Patients with gout should avoid foods high in purines, as these can increase uric acid levels. Examples include organ meats (liver, kidney, sweetbreads), red meat (beef, lamb, pork), some seafood (sardines, anchovies, mussels, scallops), and alcoholic beverages, especially beer. Sugary drinks and foods high in fructose should also be limited.

## Question: What medications are commonly prescribed for gout?

### Zero-shot response:
Commonly prescribed medications for gout include NSAIDs (like ibuprofen or naproxen) for acute attacks, colchicine to reduce inflammation, and corticosteroids (like prednisone) for severe flares. For long-term management to lower uric acid levels, allopurinol and febuxostat are frequently used.
